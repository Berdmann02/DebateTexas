About Our Camp
The Janet Melton and Sandra Green Debate Institute was created to honor to remarkable women that served the speech and debate community. They helped guide countless individuals into the field of education, specifically speech and debate. They had recruited and convinced numerous students each year to just give it a shot. Named after two women that were pivotal to the speech and debate community, specifically in Central Texas, the MGSDI aims to allow individuals the opportunity to learn and hone the craft of communication.
Sandra Greene was a big proponent of "get 'em on the bus". She figured if you just got people to that first tournament, whether it was a UIL Ready Writer, or a brand new coach, or a novice CX debater, they would be better off at the end of that day as humans than they were at the start. And while she retired, she never stopped teaching. Long after her coaching days she was a fixture in tabrooms throughout the state.
Janet Melton was an incredible proponent of speech and debate. In particular, she was a driving force behind rural debate and small school debate. Mrs. Melton brought the best out of her students competitively, but more importantly, attitudinally. For Janet, wins in speech and debate weren’t the infrastructure for the activity. Instead, Janet believed that speech and debate is a venue where students could find themselves and grow. Despite leaving this world too soon, Janet’s legacy permeates the community.
Faculty for Our Camp

Chris O'Brien

Chris is head of Instruction and Curriculum for Cross Examination Debate. He will also be assisting with the varsity CX lab. Mr. O'Brien has worked with teams that have qualified for TFA State and NCFL nationals. He has taught basic argumentation and speaking skills, and worked with all ranges of novices of varying styles of speech and debate. He competed for four years in high school, qualifying and medaling at UIL State as a quarter-finalist and semi-finalist. He qualified for TFA state both his junior and senior year, as well as NSDA. He has worked with small schools competing in UIL and TFA across the state in various events over the past five years, helping teams make appearances at various elimination rounds of UIL state, including multiple appearances at both semis and finals including both finalists in the 2022 4A UIL CX State Finals.

Kay Edwards

Kay will be working in the Varsity CX Debate Lab. Kay has been involved with speech and debate events since 2013 including competing, judging, and consulting. During her time as a competitor at North Lamar High School, she was a state finalist In Policy Debate 3 out of 4 years. She also qualified to the TFA State Tournament twice. A graduate of the University of Texas at Austin with a Bachelor of Social Work, Kay currently splits her time between the speech & debate community and accessibility work, primarily transcribing and captioning content for Deaf/HoH clients.

Melonie Menefee

Melonie will be working in the Journalism lab. She has been a journalism adviser for 27 years with Buffalo ISD, where her staff produces both the yearbook and an online newspaper.
